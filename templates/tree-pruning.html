ISA-Standard Tree Pruning in Palm Beach & Martin Counties

Improper pruning is one of the most common — and most damaging — things done to trees. Flush cuts, topping, and over-thinning create large wounds, weaken structure, and invite disease. At ArborCentric Solutions, every pruning cut follows ISA Best Management Practices, performed by Robert Castleberry, ISA Certified Arborist (#FL-6022A) and FL ISA Prescription Pruning Qualification (PPQ) holder. The PPQ is a specialized pruning credential — most tree companies don't have one.

We serve residential homeowners, HOAs, and commercial properties across Jupiter, Tequesta, Palm Beach Gardens, West Palm Beach, Wellington, Stuart, Hobe Sound, Lake Worth Beach, and all of Palm Beach and Martin Counties.

Our Tree Pruning Services Include:

  • ✓ Crown thinning — Selective removal of interior branches to increase light penetration, improve air circulation, and reduce wind resistance — critical for South Florida storm readiness
  • ✓ Crown raising — Removal of lower branches to provide clearance for pedestrians, vehicles, structures, and sight lines without compromising the tree's health
  • ✓ Crown reduction — Controlled reduction of tree height or spread using proper lateral branch cuts — never topping, which destroys structure and accelerates decline
  • ✓ Structural pruning — Training young and mid-age trees to develop a strong central leader, well-distributed branches, and sound architecture for long-term stability
  • ✓ Deadwood & hazard pruning — Removal of dead, dying, or structurally compromised branches that pose a risk to your property, family, or neighbors

Tree Pruning — Frequently Asked Questions

For most species in South Florida, late winter to early spring (February–April) is the optimal pruning window — after the coldest temperatures have passed and before the active growing season accelerates. That said, hazard pruning and deadwood removal can and should be done year-round. Pre-hurricane season pruning (April–May) is also highly recommended to reduce wind resistance before tropical storm season. Robert Castleberry will advise on the best timing for your specific species during a consultation.

Tree topping is the indiscriminate cutting of large branches to stubs or lateral branches that are too small to assume the terminal role. It's one of the most harmful practices in the tree care industry. Topping creates massive wounds the tree cannot seal, triggers rapid regrowth of weak, poorly attached "water sprouts," and dramatically shortens a tree's lifespan. ISA does not recognize topping as an acceptable pruning practice. ArborCentric Solutions never tops trees — we use crown reduction to achieve height control properly.

ISA Best Management Practices recommend removing no more than 25% of a tree's live crown in a single pruning session for most species. Removing too much foliage at once stresses the tree, depletes its energy reserves, and can trigger aggressive regrowth or accelerate decline. The exact threshold depends on the species, tree age, and current health. Robert will always work within safe limits and explain the reasoning behind every cut recommendation before work begins.

Properly executed crown thinning significantly improves a tree's wind resistance by allowing wind to pass through the canopy rather than pushing against it like a sail. Removing deadwood, crossing branches, and structurally weak limbs before storm season reduces the risk of branch failure during high-wind events. This is one of the most practical investments a Florida homeowner or HOA can make — a fallen limb or uprooted tree can cause tens of thousands of dollars in property damage.

The FL ISA Prescription Pruning Qualification (PPQ) is a specialized credential issued by the Florida Chapter of the International Society of Arboriculture. It certifies that the holder has demonstrated advanced knowledge of pruning science, tree biology, and ISA pruning standards — beyond what is required for general ISA certification. Robert Castleberry holds this qualification, meaning when he prescribes a pruning approach for your trees, it's based on verified expertise — not trial and error. Most tree services in Palm Beach County do not have a PPQ-qualified arborist on staff.
