During a consultation, Robert Castleberry (ISA Certified Arborist #FL-6022A) will perform an on-site evaluation of your trees, assessing their overall health, structural integrity, and any signs of disease, pest damage, or hazard. Depending on your needs, this can include a formal Tree Risk Assessment (TRAQ), a health diagnosis with treatment recommendations, or a full property evaluation with a written care plan. You'll leave the consultation with a clear, science-based understanding of your trees and what — if anything — needs to be done.

During your arborist consultation, Robert can advise you on local permitting requirements specific to your property and help you understand your options before any work begins.

Common warning signs include: dead or hanging branches, cracks or splits in the main trunk, fungal growth (mushrooms) at the base or on the bark, significant lean that wasn't there before, root damage from construction or soil compaction, and visible decay (hollow spots, soft wood). However, many serious structural defects are not visible to the untrained eye.
