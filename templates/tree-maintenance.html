Tree Maintenance — Frequently Asked Questions

Most trees in Palm Beach and Martin Counties benefit from a professional inspection and pruning at least once per year, ideally before hurricane season (June–November). Fast-growing species like Laurel Oaks, Slash Pines, and Queen Palms may require more frequent attention. During your consultation, Robert Castleberry will assess your specific trees and recommend a maintenance schedule based on species, age, site conditions, and your goals for the property.

Every maintenance plan is customized to your property. A typical plan includes scheduled pruning visits, health monitoring for disease and pests, soil and fertilization recommendations, mulching guidance, and a written report after each visit. For HOAs and commercial clients, we can provide formal arborist reports and documentation for insurance or property management purposes. There's no one-size-fits-all template — we build the plan around your trees and your property.

Yes — this is one of the most important reasons to maintain trees in South Florida. Overgrown, poorly structured, or deadwood-heavy trees are significantly more likely to fail during tropical storms and hurricanes. Crown thinning reduces wind resistance, structural pruning eliminates weak branch attachments, and weight reduction lowers the risk of limb failure. Properly maintained trees are more resilient and far less likely to become a liability during storm season.

Yes — trimming is a single task; maintenance is an ongoing program. Trimming typically refers to cutting back branches for appearance or clearance. Tree maintenance encompasses regular trimming, but also includes health monitoring, soil care, fertilization, pest and disease identification, and structural evaluations over time. An ISA Certified Arborist performing maintenance follows ISA pruning standards and makes decisions based on the tree's long-term health — not just how it looks after a single visit.
